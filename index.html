Welcome!

Jacob is a content writer and instructional designer with over twenty years of experience in corporate training and writing instruction in Asia and North America. He specializes in accessibility and UX through learning management systems.

In addition to two degrees in writing and instructional design, he is currently enrolled in a graduate program in technical writing and communications at the University of Minnesota to further sharpen his content writing and technical communication skills.

Jacob has over 20,000 hours of public speaking experience and has previously worked as a content writer and instructional designer with clients working in oil, nuclear energy, academia, and medicine.

He is able to manage multiple projects, consistently meet the required deadlines, and ensure all teams and clients have their needs met.

Background

I grew up on a farm in southern Minnesota where I helped my father and grandfather manage hundreds of acres of corn and soybeans. I started college when I was 16 years old at Minnesota State University, Mankato, and then transferred to Montana State University to study computer science before deciding to focus on English composition. (I enjoyed website design and programming at the time, but I really enjoyed my work writing and editing in the MSU Writing Center.)

South Korea

After graduating, I wanted to become fluent in a second language, so I found a job teaching English to children in Ulsan, South Korea (the center of the Hyundai corporate empire) and enrolled in Korean classes at Ulsan University to study before work every morning. After one year of study, my Korean teacher suggested that I move to Seoul and study at her alma mater, Seoul National University. For six amazing months, I lived with a wonderful homestay family (who took me in as their own) and spent four hours every day studying Korean language and culture.

I had originally planned on returning to America to attend graduate school, but I wanted to continue to improve my Korean-language skills. After a series of interviews, I started working as an English academic instructor at Pagoda in the Gangnam area of Seoul. For five years, I taught executives, engineers from Samsung and Hyundai, and students preparing to attend graduate school throughout the world. In addition to interviewing prospective students and writing instructional content for students and other instructors, I taught between 8-10 hours of classes each day.

Content Writing for Corporate Instruction

In 2009, I received a South Korean long-term residence visa and started the more challenging work of corporate training, content writing, and curriculum design for large South Korean companies and universities. From 2010 to 2015, I traveled throughout Seoul and the whole Korean peninsula taking on more and more work.
